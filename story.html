My story

I never planned to do this. I just kept answering.

It started with one person. Someone who had been given a diagnosis and a leaflet, and not much else. They were frightened and they had nobody to ask. So I sat with them, and I did the reading, and I stayed.

Then somebody else wrote. Then somebody else. Ten years later I am still here, and I have never charged a single person a single cent.

A friend in India

He had brain cancer. Almost his entire family had already died of some form of cancer, so he was facing it on his own. He was posting his story on page after page, and nobody was answering him.

So I sent him a private message. We swapped stories. We kept talking. I call him a good friend now, and the thing that matters most to me is not anything I told him — it is that he is not alone any more, and he has hope.

That is what this is. That is the whole thing.

Why I am asking now

Because I have started having to turn people away, and I cannot live with it.

I am not a wealthy man. There are months where the choice is groceries for my daughter and me, or the time and cost of helping the next person who writes in. My daughter comes first, every time — but the messages keep coming, and people put real faith in me, and saying nothing back is the hardest thing I do.

Ten years in, this is the first time I have ever asked anybody for help. If this work means something to you, that is what your support pays for: the time to answer, and the room to say yes instead of sorry.

— John
