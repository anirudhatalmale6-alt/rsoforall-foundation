Ten years. Never charged a soul.

Nobody should have to face this alone.

My name is John. For ten years I have sat with people facing cancer, epilepsy and chronic illness — listening, explaining their options, and connecting them to the resources they need. Wherever they are in the world. It has never cost them a cent.

Support this work I need help

I am not a doctor and I do not sell anything. What I offer is time, a listening ear, honest information, and help finding your way through.

What I actually do

Not a hotline. Not a pamphlet. A person who answers.

When somebody is newly diagnosed, or has been told there is nothing more to be done, they are usually handed a leaflet and left to it. What they need is someone who will pick up the thread and stay with them. That is the whole job.

01

I listen, properly

People write to me and tell me everything, often for the first time. No form, no triage, no waiting list. I read every word and I write back.

02

I explain the landscape

Clear, honest, stigma-free information about the options in front of you — and just as importantly, about what to be careful of and who not to trust.

03

I connect people to resources

Support programmes, patient networks, financial assistance, people who have been where you are. Ten years of knowing where to look, given away for free.

“He was posting his story on page after page and nobody was answering. So I messaged him. We are friends now. He is not alone any more, and he has hope.”
John, on a friend in India living with brain cancer

Why I am asking for help

For the first time in ten years, I am the one who needs a hand.

I have paid for all of this myself, for over a decade. I am not a wealthy man. Lately the messages have outgrown what one person can carry on his own, and I have had to start turning people away — not because I did not want to help, but because I simply could not afford to.

That is the only reason this page exists. Support here pays for the time and the work of keeping this going: answering the messages, doing the research, staying with people through the worst months of their lives, and making sure nobody who writes to me gets silence back.

I would rather tell you that plainly than dress it up.

— John
